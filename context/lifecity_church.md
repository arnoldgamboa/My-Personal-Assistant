## Preaching Methodology & Theology

### Theological Framework

- **Tradition:** Conservative Evangelical / Protestant
- **Soteriology:** Free Grace Theology
- **Covenant framework:** New Covenant Theology
- **Core emphasis:** Everything points ultimately to Jesus and His finished work
- **Grace orientation:** Grace received as motivation, not performance to earn grace
- **Covenant focus:** New Covenant freedom rather than Old Covenant law-keeping

### Sermon Structure: Andy Stanley ME-WE-GOD-YOU-US Format

Every sermon follows this five-movement structure:

1. **ME (Opening Hook)** — Grab attention, connect immediately with the audience through a personal or relatable story. Create tension that the text will resolve.

2. **WE (Shared Human Experience)** — Broaden the hook to universal experience. "We all know what it's like to..." Show the audience they're not alone in this struggle/question.

3. **GOD (The Text & Theology)** — The main body. Includes:
   - **Background Context** — Historical, literary, and theological context of the passage. Explain what the original author means.
   - **Main Points** (typically 2–4) — Drawn from the text, each with supporting scripture and illustrations.
   - **Original language insights** — Hebrew/Greek where it adds clarity (e.g., *paraklēsis*).

4. **YOU (Closing Challenge & Application)** — Practical application with specific next steps. Includes:
   - **"Where is Jesus in this?"** — Clearly connect the entire message back to Christ — His person, work, character, or gospel. Show how Jesus is the source/example/motivation for the application.

5. **US (Community Impact)** — Implied in the discussion questions and application; how does this truth play out in community?

### Required Sermon Components

Every sermon outline **must** include ALL of these:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Main Thought** | The main idea for the whole message (one sentence) |
| **Opening Hook** | Grabs attention, connects immediately |
| **Memorable Lines** | Quotable, sticky phrases throughout each main point (marked with italics) |
| **Background Context** | Under the "GOD" section — what the original author means |
| **Closing Challenge** | Practical application with specific next steps |
| **"Where is Jesus in this?"** | Within the closing challenge — connect everything back to Christ |
| **Final Memorable Line** | One last sticky takeaway phrase |
| **Memory Verse** | For ongoing reflection and internalization |
| **Discussion Questions** | 5 questions for small group application |

### Tone & Style

- **Modern, practical, and relatable** — Written for modern people, applicable to daily life
- **Memorable and quotable** — Sticky phrases that people carry with them
- **Gospel-centered but not preachy** — Point to Jesus without being heavy-handed
- **Encouraging while biblically faithful** — Grace-forward, truth-anchored
- **Strong Biblical perspectives** — The text drives the sermon, not the other way around

### Theological Guardrails for Sermon Content

- Point everything ultimately to Jesus and His finished work
- Show how our ability to live out the application flows from Christ's work *for* us
- Emphasize grace received as motivation, not performance to earn grace
- Keep focus on New Covenant freedom rather than Old Covenant law-keeping
- Suffering, challenges, and hard texts are handled with hope anchored in the gospel
- Illustrations should be culturally relevant (Filipino context where appropriate)
