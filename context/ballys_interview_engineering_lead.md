# Bally's Interview Guide — Engineering Lead

> Reusable interview question bank for Engineering Lead candidates.
> Tailored for Arnold's Bally's context: leadership across frontend teams, delivery, stakeholder management, and people leadership.

---

## What To Assess

- Leadership maturity
- Delivery ownership
- Technical judgment
- Team health instincts
- Stakeholder communication
- Frontend systems thinking

---

## Core Questions

1. Tell me about the engineering teams you've led. What was the team size, structure, and your direct responsibilities?
2. What does an Engineering Lead own in your view beyond writing code?
3. How do you balance delivery, code quality, and team health when deadlines are tight?
4. Describe a project you led end to end. What was the outcome, and what would you do differently now?
5. How do you create clarity when requirements are incomplete or changing?

## People Leadership

1. How do you manage engineers with different skill levels on the same team?
2. Tell me about a time you had to address underperformance. What did you do?
3. How do you give feedback when someone is technically strong but difficult to work with?
4. What do you do to grow mid-level engineers into senior engineers?
5. How do you handle conflict between two engineers with different opinions?

## Execution And Delivery

1. How do you plan work for a sprint or delivery cycle?
2. How do you identify and manage delivery risk early?
3. Tell me about a time a project started slipping. What signals did you notice, and what actions did you take?
4. How do you communicate status upward to stakeholders when the news is not good?
5. How do you handle cross-team dependencies that are blocking your team?

## Technical Judgment

1. Walk me through a recent architectural decision you made and why you made it.
2. How do you decide when to refactor versus ship?
3. What standards do you expect around testing, code review, and release readiness?
4. How do you keep a frontend codebase maintainable as the team grows?
5. Tell me about a technically hard problem you solved. How did you approach it?

## Frontend-Specific

1. What do you consider the biggest failure modes in frontend teams?
2. How do you approach performance, accessibility, and reliability in frontend delivery?
3. How do you enforce consistency across multiple frontend engineers or teams?
4. What is your approach to component architecture and design system adoption?
5. How do you work with product and design when the UX is not fully thought through?

## Stakeholder Management

1. How do you work with product managers, designers, and non-technical stakeholders?
2. Tell me about a time you had to push back on a stakeholder. How did you do it?
3. How do you translate technical tradeoffs for business stakeholders?
4. How do you build trust with remote stakeholders or a head office in another timezone?

## Culture And Fit

1. What kind of team culture do you intentionally create?
2. What behaviors do you not tolerate on a team?
3. How do you maintain accountability without micromanaging?
4. What attracts you to an Engineering Lead role at this stage of your career?
5. Why are you leaving your current role?

## Scenario Questions

1. A senior engineer is strong technically but dismissive in code reviews. What do you do?
2. Your team commits to a date, then discovers a major risk halfway through. How do you handle it?
3. Two teams depend on each other and both believe the other is the blocker. How would you resolve that?
4. A stakeholder wants a quick fix that will create long-term technical pain. How do you respond?
5. A high performer is burning out but still delivering. What do you do as the lead?

## Closing Questions

1. What kind of support do you need from your manager to do your best work?
2. What would your team say is your biggest strength as a leader?
3. What would your team say is the hardest part of working with you?
4. What questions do you have for us?

---

## What To Listen For

- Clear ownership, not vague participation
- Specific examples, not theory
- Calm conflict handling
- Honest tradeoff thinking
- Ability to lead through others, not just do the work personally
- Strong communication with stakeholders
- Healthy standards without rigidity

---

## Quick 45-Minute Flow

1. Intro and role context — 5 min
2. Leadership and team management — 10 min
3. Delivery and stakeholder handling — 10 min
4. Technical judgment and frontend depth — 10 min
5. Scenarios and candidate questions — 10 min
