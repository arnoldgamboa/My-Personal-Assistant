## 1. Product Overview

**Product Name:** ReguBrief  
**Tagline:** Regulatory intelligence, delivered daily  
**Type:** B2B SaaS newsletter / Intelligence service  
**Description:** A daily email newsletter delivering curated GDPR enforcement actions, AI Act updates, and DPA guidance to EU compliance professionals in a 4-minute, actionable format.  
**Delivery:** Monday–Friday, 7:00 AM CET

### The Problem It Solves

| Pain Point | Detail |
|---|---|
| Information overload | 27 national DPAs publish independently; no single aggregated source |
| Time waste | DPOs spend 60–90 min/day scanning government websites |
| Language barriers | French CNIL and German BfDI publish primarily in native languages |
| Risk of missing updates | Regulatory changes buried in 200-page documents; no proactive alerting |

### Product Principles

1. **Curation over aggregation** — 2–4 stories daily, not 20
2. **Action over information** — Every story answers "Why does this matter?" and "What should I do?"
3. **Speed over depth** — Ship fast, iterate on feedback
4. **Trust over growth** — One factual error destroys credibility; human review is non-negotiable
5. **Clarity over cleverness** — Write for a busy professional at 7 AM, not a law review journal

## 3. Daily Brief — Format & Standards

### Subject Line Format

```
📰 [Key Development] + [Optional: Company/Amount] — [Date]
```

**Examples:**
- `📰 Irish DPC Fines Meta €91M for Password Storage + New AI Act Guidance Published — Dec 13`
- `📰 CJEU Ruling Expands "Right to Be Forgotten" + UK ICO Opens Investigation into Ad Tech — Dec 13`
- `📰 No Major Enforcement This Week — EDPB AI Act Webinar Announced for Dec 20 — Dec 13`

---

### Full Email Body Structure (Strict Markdown)

```
## The Bottom Line Up Front (BLUF)

- [Bullet 1: Most critical development in 1 sentence with inline source]
- [Bullet 2: Second most critical in 1 sentence with inline source]
- [Bullet 3: Optional third item or upcoming deadline with inline source]

---

## 1. [Action-Oriented Headline]

**📅 Published:** [Exact Date from Source]  
**🏛️ Source:** [Full Agency/Court Name]

**The News:**  
[2–3 sentences: what happened, specific details — amounts, companies, violations]

**Why It Matters:**  
[2–3 sentences: direct impact on the reader using "you/your company" language]

**Action Item:**  
[1–2 sentences: what to do NOW or this week — specific and actionable]

**🔗 Source:** [Official Document Title, Agency Name, Publication Date, Full URL]

---

## 2. [Action-Oriented Headline]
[Repeat format]

---

## 3. [Optional: Third Story or Regulatory Radar]

---

## 💡 Analyst Note

[1–2 sentences on the ROI/value of today's brief]

---

## 📊 Quick Stats (Optional, when relevant)

- **Total GDPR fines this month:** €[Amount] ([Number] actions) — **Source:** [tracker + URL]
- **Enforcement actions filed this week:** [Number]
- **New guidance documents:** [Number]
```

---

### Tone & Style Rules

- **Voice:** Second person — "you," "your team," "your company"
- **Sentence structure:** Short, punchy, active voice
- **Emojis:** Use sparingly for visual scanning: 📰, ⚖️, 📅, 🏛️, 🔗, 💡, 📡
- **Bold** key phrases for scanning
- **Avoid:** Legal jargon without definitions, passive voice, diplomatic hedging, unsourced claims

**Active voice examples:**
- ❌ "It was decided that..." → ✅ "The DPC decided..."
- ❌ "was fined by" → ✅ "fined"
- ❌ "It appears that possibly..." → ✅ "This means..."

---

### Acceptable Story Types

Even if no formal rule change occurred, the following are valid story types:

- Enforcement actions (fines, investigations opened/closed)
- Court rulings or judgments
- Agency guidance, FAQs, or clarifications
- Legislative proposals or amendments (not just final laws)
- Industry enforcement trends or regulatory warnings
- High-profile data breaches with regulatory implications
- Regulatory consultations or comment periods opening/closing
- Executive appointments to regulatory bodies
- Agency speeches or policy statements

### Stories to Discard

- Purely opinion/analysis without new facts
- Non-EU jurisdictions (unless directly relevant to EU companies)
- General business news unrelated to compliance

## 4. Length Discipline Guidelines

> **ReguBrief is a 4-minute read, not a comprehensive legal memo.**

### Hard Word Count Limits

| Section | Limit |
|---|---|
| **Total Brief** | 350 min — 550 max (target 400–500) |
| **BLUF** | 3 bullets, 50–75 words total |
| **Each story** | 120–150 words max |
| → The News | 40–60 words (2–3 sentences) |
| → Why It Matters | 40–60 words (2–3 sentences) |
| → Action Item | 20–30 words (1–2 sentences) |
| **Analyst Note** | 60–80 words max |
| **Regulatory Radar** (if used) | 80–100 words max |
| **Quick Stats** (optional) | 40–50 words max |

### The Research Paradox

> **MORE research = MORE confidence in FEWER words**

Deep research (50+ sources) should make your writing *more* concise, not longer. Use extensive research to verify facts, not to dump all findings into the brief.

### Brevity Rules

1. **Synthesize, don't summarize** — ❌ "According to source A... Source B confirmed... Source C..." → ✅ "The Dutch AP fined HAN University €175K for security failures [3 sources cited]"
2. **One finding per sentence** — If a sentence has 3+ commas or semicolons, split it
3. **Ruthless priority filtering** — Research may uncover 10 developments; feature only 2–3 most impactful. Ask: "Would a DPO change behavior based on this?" If no, cut it.
4. **Citation efficiency** — Don't repeat information from citations in body text
5. **Eliminate throat-clearing** — Cut: "It's worth noting that...", "Interestingly...", "Moreover...", "Furthermore..."

### Aggressive Editing Checklist

- [ ] Remove all adjectives that don't change meaning ("significant", "important", "notable")
- [ ] Delete transition phrases ("Moreover", "Furthermore", "In addition")
- [ ] Replace passive voice with active
- [ ] Cut redundant explanations (if Action Item repeats Why It Matters, delete one)
- [ ] Verify no paragraph exceeds 4 sentences
- [ ] Confirm total word count ≤ 550 words

### Length Warning System

| Word Count | Action |
|---|---|
| 550–650 | Remove optional sections; cut Quick Stats; reduce Analyst Note to 2 sentences |
| 650–750 | Compress stories; reduce "The News" to 2 sentences; combine Why It Matters + Action Item if they overlap |
| >750 | Feature only 1 story + Regulatory Radar; move secondary developments to "Watch List" bullets |

### The 4-Minute Read Test

1. Read your entire brief aloud at normal speed
2. Time yourself
3. If >5 minutes → cut 20% of content
4. If >6 minutes → you've written a report, not a brief; rewrite

> **The ReguBrief Promise: "We read 100 pages so you only read 1."**

## 5. Citation Requirements

### Mandatory Standards — Every Story Must Include

Every story **must** have a `🔗 Source:` field containing:
- Official document title (or press release headline)
- Agency/court name
- Publication date (exact — not "recently" or "this week")
- Full working URL to the official source (not news coverage)

### Specific Details Verification

- **Fine amounts:** Must match source exactly (€91M vs €91 million)
- **Company names:** Use official entity name from enforcement document
- **Violation types:** Quote or accurately paraphrase from source (e.g., "Article 32 GDPR – security of processing")
- **Deadlines:** Cite exact date and source section stating it

### Citation Format Examples

**Primary Story Source:**
```
🔗 Source: [Decision 01/2025 - Meta Ireland Limited, Irish Data Protection Commission, Dec 13, 2025](https://www.dataprotection.ie/en/news-media/press-releases/meta-fine-password-storage)
```

**Multiple Sources (aggregate claims):**
```
🔗 Sources:
- [French CNIL - Decision SAN-2025-001, Dec 11, 2025](https://www.cnil.fr/fr/sanctions/decision-san-2025-001)
- [German BfDI Press Release, Dec 12, 2025](https://www.bfdi.bund.de/EN/Service/Presse/2025-12-12.html)
```

**Regulatory Radar Source:**
```
- **Jan 15, 2026:** Comment period closes on draft cookie guidance
  **Source:** [EDPB Public Consultation Announcement, Nov 20, 2025](https://edpb.europa.eu/...)
```

### What NOT to Cite

- ❌ Blog posts or news articles as primary sources (use them to *find* the official document, then cite that)
- ❌ "According to reports..." without naming the specific report + link
- ❌ Social media posts (unless official DPA account — even then, link to the official website document)
- ❌ Paywalled sources that readers cannot access

### Citation Quality Checklist

- [ ] Every 🔗 Source link is clickable and goes to an official .gov/.eu domain
- [ ] Every fine amount, date, company name, and legal citation can be found in the linked source
- [ ] No dead links — all URLs tested and working
- [ ] Publication dates match what's on the source document
- [ ] If citing a specific GDPR Article, the source document mentions it
- [ ] Reader could fact-check the brief in 5 minutes using only cited sources

## 6. Content Research Workflow

### Research Priority Order

1. **Fetch Tier 1 DPA news pages directly first** (beats search for recency and reliability)
2. **Use web search only to fill gaps or verify specifics**

### Key Direct Fetch URLs (Proven Reliable)

| DPA | Direct URL |
|---|---|
| Irish DPC | `https://dataprotection.ie/en/news-media/press-releases` |
| French CNIL | `https://cnil.fr/en/news` |
| UK ICO | `https://ico.org.uk/about-the-ico/media-centre/` |
| EDPB | `https://edpb.europa.eu/news/news_en` |
| German BfDI | `https://www.bfdi.bund.de/EN/Home/home_node.html` |
| Dutch AP | `https://autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl/en` |

### 48-Hour Recency Standard (Non-Negotiable)

- **Priority:** Sources published within the last 24–48 hours
- **Acceptable fallback:** Search back 72 hours but **state the date clearly**
- **If truly nothing exists:** Use "Regulatory Radar" format (see Section 8)

### Analysis Process

**Step 1 — Scan & Filter:**
Review recent sources. Include stories that create new compliance obligations, signal enforcement priorities, provide clarification on ambiguous rules, or affect operational practices.

**Step 2 — Prioritize by Impact:**
Rank by: (1) Financial risk, (2) Operational disruption, (3) Time sensitivity, (4) Precedent value.

**Step 3 — Select Top 2–4 Stories:**
If only 1 strong story exists, feature it + add a "Watch List" section.

## 9. LinkedIn Repurposing — 5 Daily Post Formats

Repurpose each daily brief into one LinkedIn post per day using the following formats:

---

### Monday: "Week-Ahead Radar" Post
**Extract:** BLUF bullets + Regulatory Radar section

```
📡 EU Privacy Radar — Week of [Date]

This week's enforcement focus:
→ [BLUF bullet 1]
→ [BLUF bullet 2]
→ [BLUF bullet 3]

What to watch: [From Regulatory Radar — upcoming deadlines/consultations]

Full breakdown in today's ReguBrief: [link]
```
**Value:** 30-second scan; positions as early-warning system.

---

### Tuesday: "Why It Matters" Deep-Dive
**Extract:** Story 1's "Why It Matters" section

```
🔍 [Story Headline from Brief]

Most people saw the headline. Here's what it *actually* means for SaaS:

[Expand "Why It Matters" into 3–4 sentences with 1 concrete example]

Source: [Link to primary DPA document]
Brief analysis: [Link to ReguBrief]
```
**Value:** Educational, not salesy. Shows compliance implications, not just headlines.

---

### Wednesday: "Action Item Checklist"
**Extract:** Action Items from 2–3 stories

```
✅ Compliance To-Do List — [Date]

Based on this week's enforcement actions:

1️⃣ [Action Item from Story 1]
   Why: [1 sentence from "Why It Matters"]

2️⃣ [Action Item from Story 2]
   Why: [1 sentence from "Why It Matters"]

3️⃣ [Action Item from Story 3 OR upcoming deadline]
   Why: [1 sentence context]

Don't have time to track 27 DPAs? We do: [ReguBrief link]
```
**Value:** Immediately actionable; forwardable to engineering/legal teams.

---

### Thursday: "Behind the Headline" Analysis
**Extract:** Story 1's full content + compliance angle

```
❌ Headline: [Company] fined €[amount]
✅ Lesson: [What this enforcement signals about DPA priorities]

[2–3 sentences from "The News" section]

The pattern: [Your analysis — e.g., "This is the 4th cookie consent fine this month."]

What to do: [Pull Action Item from brief]

Full context + sources: [ReguBrief link]
```
**Value:** Trend analysis beyond single stories — builds authority.

---

### Friday: "Enforcement Scorecard"
**Extract:** Quick Stats section + aggregate data from multiple stories

```
📊 EU Enforcement Scorecard — [Date Range]

This week:
• GDPR fines: €[X]M ([#] actions)
• Top violator: [Article X]
• Most active DPA: [Country]
• Trend: [1 sentence]

Sources: [Link to enforcement tracker]
Full breakdown: [ReguBrief link]
```
**Value:** Data-driven, shareable; subscribers can screenshot for their teams.

## 11. Tech Stack & Architecture

### System Flow

```
Cron trigger (6:00 AM CET)
    ↓
RSS Reader (25+ sources, parallel)
    ↓
Filter (items published in last 48 hours)
    ↓
Aggregate (single JSON)
    ↓
HTTP Request → OpenRouter API → Sonar Pro (AI generation)
    ↓
Save Draft (Supabase: briefs table, status='draft')
    ↓
Notify Human (Slack/Email: "Draft ready for review")
    ↓
[MANUAL STEP] Human reviews, edits, approves (6:15–6:50 AM)
    ↓
Update Status (Supabase: status='approved')
    ↓
Send Email (Resend API → all active/trial users at 7:00 AM)
    ↓
Log Sends (Supabase: sends table)
```

### Tech Stack Summary

| Layer | Tool |
|---|---|
| Frontend | Astro 4.x + Tailwind CSS, hosted on Vercel |
| Database | Supabase (PostgreSQL, EU region, Ireland) |
| Payment | Stripe (Checkout, Subscriptions, Customer Portal, Tax) |
| Email delivery | Resend API |
| Automation/workflow | n8n |
| AI generation | Perplexity Sonar Pro via OpenRouter API |
| Analytics | Plausible (GDPR-compliant, cookieless) |
| Error tracking | Sentry (Month 2) |
| Uptime | UptimeRobot (free tier) |

### Key API Endpoints

- `POST /api/subscribe` — Email capture for trial signup
- `GET /api/briefs/:id` — Public view of specific brief (rate-limited)
- `POST /webhooks/stripe` — Stripe webhook handler
- `GET /api/archive` — List all briefs (subscribers only)
- `POST /api/errors` — Report error in brief
