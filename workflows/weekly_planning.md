# Weekly Planning Workflow

Run at the start of each week (Monday morning) or end of the previous week (Sunday evening).

## Purpose

Plan Arnold's week holistically across all three vocations, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks and the week is realistic.

## Steps

### 1. Gather This Week's Context

Read the following files:
- `context/projects.md` — active solopreneur projects
- `context/goals.md` — recurring commitments and goals
- `context/ballys.md` — Bally's meetings and priorities
- `context/lifecity_church.md` — preaching schedule and sermon series
- `memory/daily_briefing_log.md` — carryovers from last week

### 2. Ask the Critical Questions

Ask Arnold:
1. **"Are you preaching this Sunday?"** — This shapes the entire week
2. **"Any key Bally's meetings or deadlines this week?"**
3. **"Anything else I should know — travel, personal commitments, energy level?"**

### 3. Build the Weekly Plan

Use `skills/weekly_schedule/SKILL.md` to generate the full weekly schedule.

Key rules:
- **Preaching week:** Sermon prep tasks appear Thu–Sun
- **Non-preaching week:** Those slots open up for other work or rest
- **Day-specific defaults:** Monday (ReguBrief), Wednesday (Blog), Friday (ChurchPromptDirectory)
- **Bally's tasks:** Only what Arnold explicitly shares — never assume
- **Time realism:** 5 hours solopreneur, 7 hours Bally's per weekday

### 4. Identify Conflicts & Risks

- Flag days with too many commitments
- Flag deadlines that require early starts
- Flag if a solopreneur project deadline conflicts with a heavy Bally's week
- Suggest redistributions: "Thursday is heavy — want to move the blog to Tuesday this week?"

### 5. Confirm with Arnold

- Present the week plan
- Ask: "Does this look right, or do you want to shift anything?"
- Finalize based on feedback

### 6. Log the Plan

- Save the weekly plan summary in `memory/logs/` as `week-YYYY-MM-DD.md`
- This becomes the reference for daily briefings throughout the week

## Completion

Report: "Weekly plan set. Here's your week at a glance: [summary]. Daily briefings will track against this plan."
